Master the cold call
in three moments.

Ethan is 24, sceptical, and didn't ask for your call.
Your only goal: book a session with him.

🚪
The Door
Every call
Get in. Name, company, one reason it might matter to him, ask for 20 seconds. One breath — don't pause in the middle.
Try this
"Hi Ethan, Alvin here — I'm a financial representative from Great Eastern. I help people who just started working make sure their income is actually protected. Got 20 seconds?"
🎯
The Hook
Sometimes
If Ethan gives you space after the opener — don't pitch. Share one observation anchored to where he actually is right now. Then ask one light question and stop talking.
Try this
"Most people your age are earning well but spending everything they earn — no buffer if something changes. Is that something you've thought about?"
✋
The Exit
Almost every call — Move 1
He pushes back right after the Door. Don't explain. Don't pitch. Ask for just one moment and stop talking. Let him ask what it is.
Say exactly this
"Before you go — just one thing."
Then stop. Silence. Wait for him to ask "What one thing?"
💡
The Mini Hook
Almost every call — Move 2
He asked "What one thing?" Now you have one sentence to make it worth his time. One specific insight about people in his situation. Not the Ask yet — re-earn the right first.
Try this
"Most people your age haven't checked if their company insurance covers them if they can't work — it usually doesn't once you leave. That's usually why it's worth a look."
Now ask for the session.
📅
The Ask
Every call — sometimes twice
No commitment, name the session, how long, two specific times. All in one breath. If he pushes back again — agree, then close with the same two options.
Try this
"Nothing to commit to — just a quick review of your current situation. About 20 minutes. Tuesday 8pm or Saturday morning?"
Watch one first.
Three ways a call can go. Tap to switch.
Most calls go this way.

Ethan

About this prospect

Ethan is fresh into the workforce and lives with his parents. He has a job, some savings, and company insurance — and he thinks that is enough for now. Financial planning feels like something for people with families or mortgages. Not him. Not yet.

What to expect

He picks up but may want to end the call fast. Give him one good reason to stay on the line. He responds to language that connects to where he actually is right now — not where he will be in 30 years.

💡 Your only goal: book a short session. Not to explain everything — just to get the meeting.
